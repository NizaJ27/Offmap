Lebanon

From Beirut’s buzz to mountain villages, discover Lebanon’s rich culture.

All Destinations | Contact
What you can do in Lebanon: Stroll vibrant city streets, hike dramatic mountain valleys, savor mezze by the sea, and explore ancient ruins. Lebanon is a crossroads of cultures—ideal for adventurers, foodies, and history lovers.

Beirut Corniche at Sunset

Location: Beirut Corniche (seafront promenade)

What to expect: Locals jogging, families strolling, and fishermen casting lines. The Mediterranean breeze and city skyline make for a perfect evening walk.

Personal experience: I joined a group of locals for coffee on the rocks, sharing stories as the sun dipped below the horizon.

Village Souks & Manakish

Location: Deir el Qamar, Byblos, or any mountain village

What to expect: Bustling markets, fresh produce, and the smell of baking bread. Try manakish (za’atar flatbread) straight from a wood-fired oven.

Personal experience: I watched a baker in Byblos spin dough in the air, then shared a warm manakish with a local family—hospitality here is legendary.

Byblos Ruins & Seaside Cafes

Location: Byblos (Jbeil), north of Beirut

What to expect: Ancient ruins, cobbled streets, and open-air cafes overlooking the sea. History and relaxation in one spot.

Personal experience: I spent an afternoon exploring Phoenician ruins, then sipped mint lemonade at a cafe with a view of the harbor.

Hiking the Qadisha Valley

Location: Qadisha Valley, northern Lebanon

What to expect: Dramatic cliffs, ancient monasteries, and breathtaking views. Trails range from easy walks to challenging hikes.

Personal experience: Hiking through the valley, I met a shepherd who pointed out hidden caves and shared homemade cheese—an unforgettable encounter.
