Lisbon

Sunny, soulful, and full of hidden gems. Discover the best of Portugal’s capital.

All Destinations | Contact
What you can do in Lisbon: Wander pastel-colored streets, ride historic trams, savor world-famous pastries, and soak up sunset views from hilltop miradouros. Lisbon is a city where old-world charm meets creative energy—perfect for foodies, explorers, and culture lovers.

Alfama & Fado Nights

Location: Alfama district (near São Jorge Castle)

What to expect: Lose yourself in winding alleys, tiled facades, and the soulful sounds of live fado music drifting from cozy taverns. Alfama is the heart of old Lisbon, and every corner feels like a story.

Personal experience: My favorite night in Lisbon was spent in a tiny fado bar, sipping vinho verde and listening to a singer pour her heart out. The emotion in the room was unforgettable.

Tram 28 Adventure

Location: Martim Moniz to Campo de Ourique (Tram 28 route)

What to expect: Hop on the iconic yellow tram for a rickety, scenic ride through Lisbon’s hills, passing historic neighborhoods and stunning viewpoints. Go early to avoid crowds!

Personal experience: I rode Tram 28 at sunrise and had the carriage almost to myself. Watching the city wake up from those wooden seats is pure magic.

Pastéis de Nata Hunt

Location: Pastéis de Belém, Rua de Belém 84-92

What to expect: Taste the original custard tart at its birthplace, then try local bakeries for a less-touristy (but equally delicious) version. Expect a crispy shell, creamy filling, and a dusting of cinnamon.

Personal experience: I did a “nata crawl” across the city—my top pick was a tiny bakery in Graça, where the owner handed me a warm tart straight from the oven.

Miradouro da Senhora do Monte

Location: Largo Monte, Graça

What to expect: One of Lisbon’s best viewpoints. Bring a picnic, watch the sunset, and see the city’s rooftops glow gold.

Personal experience: I brought a pastel de nata and a sketchbook, and spent an hour just soaking in the view. It’s a peaceful spot even in high season.
