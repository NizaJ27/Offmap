Chiang Mai

Northern Thailand’s creative heart, with food, culture, and adventure.

All Destinations | Contact
What you can do in Chiang Mai: Explore ancient temples, feast at bustling night markets, take a cooking class, and escape to lush mountains. Chiang Mai is a haven for digital nomads, foodies, and anyone seeking a blend of tradition and creativity.

Morning Markets & Street Food

Location: Warorot Market, Chang Moi Road

What to expect: Dive into a sensory overload of sights, smells, and flavors. Try khao soi, mango sticky rice, and fresh fruit smoothies from local vendors.

Personal experience: My best breakfast was a bowl of khao soi at a tiny stall, shared with locals who insisted I try their favorite chili paste.

Temple Hopping

Location: Old City (Wat Phra Singh, Wat Chedi Luang)

What to expect: Golden stupas, chanting monks, and peaceful courtyards. Each temple has its own story and unique atmosphere.

Personal experience: I spent a quiet afternoon sketching at Wat Phra Singh, and a monk came over to chat about meditation and travel.

Cooking Class Adventure

Location: Various schools (many offer hotel pickup)

What to expect: Learn to make Thai classics from scratch, visit a local market, and enjoy a feast you cooked yourself.

Personal experience: I made pad thai and green curry, and the chef’s stories about her grandmother’s recipes made the day extra special.

Yi Peng Lantern Festival

Location: Along the Ping River and city temples (November)

What to expect: Thousands of lanterns light up the night sky in a magical, unforgettable celebration. Arrive early for the best views.

Personal experience: Releasing a lantern with new friends was a highlight of my travels—everyone cheered as the sky filled with light.
