New York

The city that never sleeps—hidden eats, art, and neighborhoods await.

All Destinations | Contact
What you can do in New York: Walk iconic neighborhoods, discover world-class art, eat your way through every borough, and catch a show or street performance. New York is a city of endless energy and surprises—perfect for explorers, artists, and food lovers.

The High Line

Location: Gansevoort St. to 34th St. (Elevated park on Manhattan’s West Side)

What to expect: A unique park built on a former rail line, with gardens, art installations, and city views. Great for a morning stroll or sunset walk.

Personal experience: I grabbed a coffee and wandered the High Line at sunrise—watching the city wake up from above is unforgettable.

Brooklyn Delis & Eats

Location: Williamsburg, Park Slope, and beyond

What to expect: Classic delis, bagels, and pizza joints. Each neighborhood has its own flavor—don’t miss the local bakeries!

Personal experience: I found the best bagel of my life in a tiny shop in Park Slope, where the owner knew every customer by name.

Central Park & Bryant Park

Location: Central Park (59th–110th St.), Bryant Park (42nd St. & 6th Ave.)

What to expect: Free concerts, movie nights, and people-watching. Both parks are green oases in the city, perfect for relaxing or joining a local event.

Personal experience: I stumbled upon a jazz concert in Bryant Park and ended up picnicking with new friends until dusk.

Bushwick & LES Street Art

Location: Bushwick (Brooklyn), Lower East Side (Manhattan)

What to expect: Colorful murals, graffiti, and creative spaces. Take a self-guided tour or join a local art walk.

Personal experience: I spent an afternoon photographing murals in Bushwick, then found a pop-up gallery in an old warehouse—NYC’s art scene is always evolving.
